Let’s Step up the Act

Dear Changemaker

It is past half-time for SDG4. Progress is slow. The problems that were before the Covid with 52% of children unable to read, remains largely unchanged. Many countries have yeat to reach the Pre-Covid levels. A quality education is implausible without first addressing The problem of foundational literacy and numeracy (FLN).

A Crisis of Pedagogy

The much ignored fact is the crises of pedagogy. The crisis in learning is a crisis of pedagogy; both how we teach the foundational skills, and how quickly we teach them. Rote methods of teaching and learning are holding hostage enormous potential of the children, many of whom drop out of schools, and suffer indignation the rest of their lives.

How will the world convert its well-intentioned resolves into reality, like those made last September at the UN Transforming Education Summit in New York? What will the pathway to universal literacy be? When will foundational skills include climate change as their essential component now before COP 28? What new pedagogies and policies will it take to deliver foundational skills within weeks and months, and not years?

Research Spotlight

Themes

FLN Landscape and Research Trends

Explore the current FLN research landscape, emphasizing the imperative of research for SDG4 and Foundational Literacy and Numeracy (FLN). Delve into the latest trends, challenges, and transformative opportunities shaping reading, writing, and arithmetic skills.

Research, Practice, and Technology in FLN

Discuss the seamless integration of research findings into impactful classroom practices. Explore demonstrations and discussions on leveraging technology to narrow the digital divide in FLN.

Diversity and Evidence-Based FLN Practices

Strategize diversity, equity, and inclusion in educational settings, particularly focusing on the education of girls and women. Emphasize evidence-based FLN practices with a focus on scalability and sustainability

Teacher Training and Assessment for FLN Impact

Discover research-backed strategies for training educators in effective FLN instruction. Explore assessment methods for measuring impact and progress, providing meaningful insights.

Closing Session: Global Collaboration for FLN Advancement

Global Ed Innovator Fellowships & Awards

Fellowships and Awards are our way of saying: “Thank you for being a part of the change process so urgently needed in education.” Fellowships are for promising ideas for research and innovations-in-action. Awards are for programs which are already able to demonstrate their impact with evidence.

INTERNATIONAL JURY

An international jury will make the selections based on transparent criteria to award between 100 to 200 Fellowships this year depending upon the quality of the submissions.

FELLOWSHIPS

All GETI Fellows are expected to carry out their ideas for change post selection, and to gather evidence of their implementation on a regular basis in an e-portfolio. Evidence includes photos, videos, slide presentations, surveys, pre-and post-intervention data and its analysis (quantitative, anecdotal and qualitative) as in action research.

All GETI Fellows with evidence will automatically qualify for the Global Ed Innovator Awards.
