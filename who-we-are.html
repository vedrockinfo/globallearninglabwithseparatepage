City Montessori School, Lucknow World’s Largest School Recipient UNESCO Prize for Peace Education

As the largest school in the world, City Montessori School (CMS), has always upheld the banner of quality education and has promoted an education based on principles of equity and excellence.

A K12 school in the city Lucknow, India, CMS started in 1959 with 5 children and the equivalent of 10 dollars in borrowed capital by Drs. Jagdish and Bharti Gandhi. The school was entered in the Guinness Book of World Records in 1999 and has since maintained its position as the world's largest school with the current enrollment of 61,000 children. CMS has promoted an education for peace and world unity. It received the UNESCO Prize for Peace Education in 2002. Children and staff greet each other "Jai Jagat! ‘Let glory be for the entire world’!

CMS hosts the Conference of Chief Justices of the World.

Accelerating Learning for AllPedagogy of the Future Today

ALfA, or Accelerating Learning for All, revolutionizes education with a child-led focus on foundational literacy and numeracy. Given the stark fact that 2 out of 3 children under ten lack basic skills, ALfA seeks to correct educational failures. This innovative pedagogy ensures rapid literacy and numeracy acquisition in just 45 days, prioritizing effectiveness, joy, and well-being.

Going beyond conventional learning, ALfA strives to offer holistic education for every child. Envisioning a collaborative and conflict-free future, ALfA equips children with skills to shape a climate conscious peaceful world free from poverty—a crucial approach for a brighter global reality.
